The name curry comes from “kari”, the Tamil word for “sauce”. Ironically “cury” is also medieval English for “cooking” although these days curries don’t need to have sauce. They can be both “wet” and “dry”. The one constant is the presence of spices and other aromatics. There is archaeological evidence that people of the subcontinent were pounding spices such as mustard seeds, fennel seeds, cumin and tamarind pods in crude mortar and pestles over 4000 years ago to flavour food.
